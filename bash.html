Bash,

When you first stole me away from GUI, I must admit that I was a little hesitant at the beginning. You may not have the looks that Sublime had, but I shortly figured out that your appearance is easy to change to align with my Preferences. But I guess everyone goes through a GUI phase, and I shouldn’t just judge a shell by its boring background and text colors…

Despite your looks though, you are much more useful than GUI, and I’ve learned so much from you. Your pwd command taught me to always live in the present (working directory). Your ls command taught me that it’s helpful to list everything that you have in the present (working directory) so you don’t make a mistake. And your cd command taught me that it’s okay to change (directories). I also appreciate how open youAreWithMe.html !

I think what was most touch ing though was when I realized you would do anything for me, like creating files from the simplest commands. You made a whole directory for me with a mere mkdir command. You remember nano details, like when I create and edit a html file. I don’t even need a dog anymore because you will even "fetch" (files to a server) for me with the command ssh !

Bash, you’re growing on me,


Lauren
